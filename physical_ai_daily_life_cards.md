# Physical AI in Daily Life — Final Card Taxonomy

This document defines the final research-card list for an interactive atlas of **physical AI in daily life**. Cards are grouped into four categories:

1. **Embodiment** — the physical aspect of interaction: form, sensors, scale, situatedness, spatial relation, expression, and control.
2. **Intelligence** — the computational capability: models, modalities, context, memory, agency, learning, uncertainty, and data/privacy behavior.
3. **Function** — the lived interaction: how people use the system across time, place, attention, trust, social configuration, and ritual.
4. **Genealogy** — the cultural, historical, market, and intellectual lineage: where the interaction comes from and what world it belongs to.

Within each category, cards are ordered by practical importance for understanding **physical AI in everyday environments** such as homes, streets, transit, workplaces, schools, care settings, and personal routines.

## 1. Embodiment

The **Embodiment** category describes what physical thing the AI becomes: its body, sensors, outputs, relationship to the human body, relationship to space, and mode of being controlled.

**Description:**  
Maps the major physical forms that daily-life AI takes: glasses, pins, watches, companion objects, appliances, speakers, desktop agents, mobile robots, humanoids, vehicles, furniture, and room-scale systems. This is one of the most important cards because form immediately shapes user expectations: a pair of glasses implies first-person sensing, a robot implies agency, and a lamp implies ambient presence.

- Rabbit R1
- Amazon Echo / Echo Show
- Google Nest Hub
- Jibo

- Figure AI humanoid
- Tesla Optimus
- 1X Neo

**Research emphasis:**  
Focus on form factor, social legibility, scale, body relationship, and what kind of interaction each form invites.

### 1.2 Formless → Form Specific

**Description:**  
Places physical AI on a spectrum from minimally embodied systems to highly form-specific agents. A voice assistant in a speaker is less form-specific than a robot with eyes, limbs, and expressive movement. A distributed smart home can be physically present but difficult to locate as a single “body.”

**Potential projects / examples:**
- Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant as mostly formless / voice-first agents
- Amazon Echo as weakly embodied voice appliance
- Echo Show / Nest Hub as screen-bodied assistant
- Rabbit R1 as dedicated handheld AI object
- Humane AI Pin as wearable-but-minimal body
- Jibo as expressive desktop body
- Moxie as character-specific robot
- LOVOT / Aibo as creature-specific forms
- Tesla Optimus / Figure AI as human-body-specific forms
- SmartThings / Home Assistant as distributed, environment-level embodiment

**Research emphasis:**  
How much does the physical form constrain, clarify, or overpromise the AI’s role?

**Potential projects / examples:**

- Echo Show / Nest Hub screen output
- Philips Hue / smart lights as ambient light actuation

## 2. Intelligence

The **Intelligence** category describes what computational capability the interaction depends on: what the system can perceive, infer, remember, generate, control, delegate, personalize, and safely handle.

### 2.1 Context Awareness

**Description:**  
The system’s ability to understand situation: where it is, who is present, what activity is happening, what objects are nearby, what time it is, what the user is trying to do, and whether intervention is appropriate. This is arguably the core intelligence of daily-life physical AI.

**Potential projects / examples:**
- VisionClaw always-on smart-glasses agent
- Ray-Ban Meta multimodal AI
- Google Lens / visual search in daily environments
- Apple Vision Pro spatial context features
- Nest thermostats occupancy and routine sensing
- Ring / Nest cameras detecting people, packages, pets
- smart eldercare monitoring systems
- ambient intelligence research for activities of daily living
- Microsoft Kinect activity recognition
- context-aware smart home research
- Home Assistant automations triggered by occupancy / time / device state

**Research emphasis:**  
What kind of context does the AI infer, and how does that inference change timing, action, or response?

**Potential projects / examples:**
- Rewind / Limitless Pendant
- Ray-Ban Meta memory-like visual assistant features

What is remembered, for whom, for how long, and under whose control?

### 2.6 Uncertainty / Confidence / Failure Handling

**Description:**  
Examines how physical AI behaves when perception, inference, or action is uncertain. Daily-life AI must handle ambiguous speech, occluded objects, multiple people, noisy homes, incomplete context, unsafe actions, and mismatched expectations.

**Potential projects / examples:**
- smart speakers mishearing commands
- autonomous vehicle disengagement as high-stakes precedent
- robot vacuum stuck / cliff / obstacle failure modes
- humanoid robots asking for clarification before acting
- assistive robots that confirm before moving objects
- smart home systems with false positives or missed events
- fall detection systems and false alarms
- wearable AI asking clarifying questions
- LLM agents with confidence / refusal / escalation patterns

**Research emphasis:**  
Look at repair, clarification, graceful degradation, handoff, and “do nothing” as interaction designs.

**Description:**  
Distinguishes what the AI is fundamentally doing: recognizing a state, predicting what will happen, generating media or language, making a decision, calling a tool, controlling a device, or physically acting in the world.

## 3. Function

**Potential projects / examples:**
- Apple Watch glanceable notifications
- smart lights as momentary feedback

- Roomba scheduled cleaning cycles

How long is the user engaged, and does the AI remain socially present after the interaction ends?

## 4. Genealogy

The **Genealogy** category describes the worlds that physical AI inherits from: historical precedents, markets, cultural references, design traditions, research communities, and earlier interaction paradigms.

### 4.1 Home Robots

**Potential projects / examples:**
- iRobot Roomba

- Hello Robot Stretch

- window-cleaning robots
- home humanoid concepts

**Research emphasis:**  
Track the shift from single-purpose domestic robots to expressive, agentic, and potentially general-purpose home robots.

### 4.2 Home Assistants

- Google Assistant
- Samsung Bixby / SmartThings
- Home Assistant

- Alexa skills

---

### 4.3 Ubiquitous Computing

**Description:**  
The research lineage that imagines computation embedded throughout everyday environments rather than concentrated in desktop computers. It grounds many physical AI ideas: distributed sensors, calm interfaces, mobile devices, location-aware systems, and smart environments.

**Potential projects / examples:**
- Mark Weiser’s ubiquitous computing vision
- Xerox PARC ubicomp work
- tabs, pads, boards as historical ubicomp artifacts
- location-aware computing research
- smart room research
- smart home living labs
- mobile and wearable computing
- sensor networks
- tangible user interfaces as adjacent lineage
- Home Assistant / IoT as contemporary consumer continuation

**Research emphasis:**  
Physical AI inherits ubicomp’s ambition: computation disappears into daily life, but now with stronger perception, agency, and generative intelligence.

### 4.7 Context-Aware Computing
