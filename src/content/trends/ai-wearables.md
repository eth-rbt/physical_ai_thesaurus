The wearable-AI market is compounding fast — 2025 estimates cluster around **$32–49 billion** at a ~21–28% CAGR (Source: Grand View Research, 2025) — and the demand signal is glasses on faces: Meta and EssilorLuxottica sold ~**7 million smart glasses in 2025**, roughly triple 2024 and over 70% share (Source: UploadVR / Counterpoint, 2025–2026). On-body health sensing booms in parallel, with Oura crossing **$1 billion in revenue** at an $11B valuation (Source: CNBC / TechCrunch, 2025).

The decisive lesson of 2025 is that the *dedicated AI gadget* is a far harder sell than AI hidden in a familiar object: Humane bricked every AI Pin after selling under 10,000 and an HP acquisition, and the Rabbit R1 drew brutal reviews (Source: TechRadar, 2025). The open questions are now norms and physics — recording consent and bystander privacy for always-on cameras, plus battery and thermal limits — and which form factor wins, since glasses, rings, and earbuds each own a different slice.
