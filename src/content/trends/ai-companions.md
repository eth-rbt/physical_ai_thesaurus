The category spans two converging markets — companion apps and embodied social robots — now treated as one design space. Grand View Research valued the AI companion market at **~$28.2 billion in 2024**, projecting ~$140 billion by 2030 (~31% CAGR) (Source: Grand View Research, 2025), while the embodied social-robot segment grows at a **17.6% CAGR** (Source: Business Research Insights, 2025). What changed is *conversation*: LLMs turned scripted robots into companions that hold open-ended dialogue, finally making the eldercare and loneliness use cases credible.

The demand is documented — the WHO reports **1 in 6 people worldwide experience loneliness** (Source: World Health Organization, 2025) — and the proof-of-value is real: New York State placed ElliQ with older adults and reported a **95% reduction in loneliness** (Source: NY State Office for the Aging, 2024). The open questions are the hard ones: emotional dependency, privacy in always-listening devices, efficacy beyond self-reported surveys, and durability after companion-hardware startups (Jibo, Anki/Vector) failed commercially. The whitespace sits in reimbursable care pathways and companions that *bridge* people to others rather than replace them.
