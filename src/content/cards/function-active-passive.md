Initiative is what separates a tool you operate from an agent that operates on you, and the spread here is less about intelligence than about nerve. At the reactive end nothing moves until you do — an Echo dead until its wake-word, a Lifeline pendant waiting for a finger, Ray-Ban glasses that only answer when summoned — and notably these waiters are the least AI-embedded of the set, content to relay rather than infer. Pushing rightward, ambient pieces like the Dangling String and Little Signals do volunteer information, but quietly and without demand, while a scheduled Roomba and an Apple Watch nudging you to stand begin to act on a clock or a threshold rather than a request. The proactive extreme is where embedded learning earns its keep: a Nest thermostat and Ring doorbell infer a pattern or a person and decide on their own, and Google Now and ElliQ go furthest — surfacing your commute unbidden or initiating a check-in call — turning the question from "what did you ask?" into "should it have spoken at all?"
