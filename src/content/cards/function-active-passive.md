Initiative separates a tool you operate from an agent that operates on you, and the telling thing is that it doesn't track intelligence. The two most reactive picks split on AI entirely — Lifeline has none and Dragon NaturallySpeaking runs core speech models — yet both simply wait, because deference, not capability, sets their place. Initiative instead climbs with authorship and inference: IFTTT and SmartThings act on rules a person wrote, while Nest crosses a real line by inferring its own schedule after a week of watching. The proactive extreme is where it gets pointed — Google Now predicts and pushes your commute unbidden, and ElliQ goes furthest by initiating a relationship rather than a task, turning the question from "what did you ask?" into "should it have spoken first at all?"
