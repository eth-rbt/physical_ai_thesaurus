Manipulation begins on the 1961 GM line with Unimate, the first industrial arm, and gains intelligence with SRI's Shakey (1966), which fused planning with physical action. Willow Garage's PR2 (2010) made household manipulation a credible research agenda and spawned ROS; Hello Robot's Stretch shrank a mobile manipulator to living-room scale; and the leading edge — Figure 02's dexterous hands and Physical Intelligence's π-0 foundation model — marks the shift from single-task arms to generalist policies that fold laundry, assemble boxes, and bus tables.
