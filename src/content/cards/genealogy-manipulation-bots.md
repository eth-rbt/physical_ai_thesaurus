Read in order, these projects trace where the knowledge of how to grasp actually lives. Unimate (1961) had real dexterity and no intelligence — a human walked it through positions it replayed off a drum — and Shakey added the reasoning mind years before the hands could act on it. The middle of the set is about reach and access: PR2 made manipulation a shared, reproducible research agenda through ROS, while Stretch and Toyota's HSR shrank the arm into living rooms and eldercare and Bot Handy held out the still-unbuilt promise of fragile-object dexterity. Only at the end, with Optimus's learned hands and Physical Intelligence's π-0 trained across eight robot bodies, does the knowing finally move inside the machine as a policy that transfers rather than code a person wrote for one task.
