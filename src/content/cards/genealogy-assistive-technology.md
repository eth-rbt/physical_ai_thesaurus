Read by date, the set tells a quieter story than its branches do: AI arrived late. The founding artifacts run none — Lifeline (1974) is a button wired to a phone line, with a human at the other end deciding everything — and the Kurzweil machine's 1976 OCR was machine intelligence's first real foothold here. Only the recent vision tools (OrCam, Seeing AI) and Dragon's dictation are AI to the core, and what the AI does is *perceive on the user's behalf*: read the page, name the face, hear the speech. The branches still diverge sharply in what "help" means — Stretch reaches and grasps, SenseCam reconstructs memory, PARO does nothing but be petted — but each poses the same bargain, whether the device extends a person's agency or quietly substitutes for it.
