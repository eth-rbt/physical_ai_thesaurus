What these projects reveal together is that the human shape stays fixed for sixty years while the reason keeps changing underneath it. WABOT-1 set the agenda and Cog made the strongest case — that the body is the precondition for the mind — but the line immediately forks: Atlas and ASIMO chase locomotion, Kismet, Pepper, Furhat, and Sophia chase a legible social face, and NAO just becomes the cheap substrate everyone experiments on. The recurring tension is that the form keeps promising more intelligence than runs inside it — Disney's 1964 Lincoln fooled audiences on tape tones with no AI at all, Sophia's lifelike face implies a generality it lacks, and Tesla's Optimus is still betting, not delivering.
