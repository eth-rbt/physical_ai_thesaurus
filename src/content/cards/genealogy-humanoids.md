The humanoid lineage starts with Waseda's WABOT-1 (1973), the first full-scale robot to walk, grip, see, and speak, and runs through Honda's ASIMO — the friendly child-sized symbol of the form for two decades — to Boston Dynamics' Atlas, which redefined bipedal agility. Its social branch (Kismet's expressive head, SoftBank's Pepper, Hanson's Sophia) chases relationship and spectacle, while the current frontier (Tesla Optimus, Figure 02) bets that anthropomorphic bodies trained by neural networks can finally take on general-purpose physical labor.
