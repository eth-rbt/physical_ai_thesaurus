The striking thing about this set is how little of it contains any AI. Weiser's 1991 vision and Brown's calm-technology law set the goal — computing recedes into the room and lives at the edge of attention — and the working ancestors deliver on it with sensing and wiring alone: X10 sends commands down house power lines, the ParcTab and Active Badge locate you by infrared cell, MediaCup trips thresholds, the Dangling String is a motor on an Ethernet cable, and Smart Dust pushes disappearance to cubic-millimeter motes that only condition signals. Across all of it the intelligence is in the woven-together environment, not any one node. Only at the far end — Things That Think as envisioned vision, Cordyceps as a shipped multi-agent system — does a mind finally inhabit the ecology, which is exactly when the lineage stops being calm and starts being able to act on its own.
