Ubiquitous computing began as Mark Weiser's 1991 claim that the most profound technologies disappear into the environment, prototyped at Xerox PARC as tabs, pads, and boards. Three decades on, that vision lives in the living room — smart-home platforms like SmartThings and Home Assistant, the Matter standard that ties them together, ambient objects like Little Signals, and networked AI agents like Cordyceps.
