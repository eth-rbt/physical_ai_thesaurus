Read together, these projects show that "creative collaborator" has almost always meant putting expression into the hands and body rather than into a model: reacTable's tangible synth pucks, musicBottles' uncorked melodies, Topobo's recorded-and-replayed motion, and Videoplace's full-body silhouettes all turn gesture and grasp into musical, kinetic, or performative output, while Illuminating Clay and Urp let designers sculpt landscapes and cities that the system reads back as projected analysis. The striking split is the AI-embedded one — almost every member here carries ai:none, the intelligence living in rule-based mappings and the maker's own hands (inFORM rendering remote touch as a rising pin-field, Siftables as shufflable tiles), so that the two genuine AI-core cases stand out: Be the Beat's deep-learning system that turns dance into generated music, and Google Photos quietly authoring montages and edits from your archive. The tension is whether the machine amplifies the body's expression or starts to do the expressing for you.
