The striking thing about this set is the AI-embedded split: almost every "creative collaborator" here runs no AI at all. From Videoplace's 1975 silhouettes through reacTable's synth pucks, musicBottles' uncorked melodies, Topobo's hand-taught gaits, Illuminating Clay's sculpted terrain, Siftables' shuffled tiles, inFORM's pin-field, and BitDrones' midair swarm, the intelligence lives in rule-based mappings and the maker's own hands — the machine amplifies the body's expression without ever doing the expressing. That makes the two genuine AI-core outliers do the arguing: Be the Beat's deep-learning read of a dancer still amplifies the body, but Google Photos crosses the line, authoring the montage and the edit itself. The question the set forces is exactly that line — whether a creative system extends what your hands can do, or quietly takes the making over.
