Along the axis the same impulse to assist keeps changing how hard it commits to a body. At the formless end Siri is a placeless voice, SmartThings dissolves into a whole-house sensor ecology, and Weiser's ubiquitous computing is the deliberate ideal of intelligence that never coheres into a thing you can address. Then form gathers: the Echo Show grows a screen-face on a swivel, the Rabbit R1 condenses into a handheld, and Nabaztag and Jibo become small characters with ears or a turning face. At the form-specific end the body makes a promise the intelligence has to keep — and the telling split is that aibo and LOVOT wrap only modest onboard ML in maximal creature expressiveness, getting away with it because a pet promises affection rather than competence, while Figure 02 takes full human shape and so promises it can do anything a person can, staking that legibility on a vision-language-action model that actually has to deliver on the silhouette.
