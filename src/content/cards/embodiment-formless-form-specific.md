Read along the axis, the same assistant intelligence keeps changing how hard it commits to a body: Siri is a placeless voice, Alexa a weakly embodied speaker, and SmartThings has no locatable form at all, present only as a distributed house — then the Echo Show and Nest Hub grow a screen-face, the Humane Pin and Rabbit R1 condense into objects you wear or hold, and Jibo and Moxie become characters that swivel and emote. At the form-specific end the body makes a promise the AI must keep: aibo and LOVOT are unmistakable creatures whose modest onboard ML is wrapped in maximal expressiveness, while Figure 02 and Tesla Optimus take human shape and thereby imply they can do anything a person can — and where the formless voices read as ai:unknown utilities, these humanoids stake their whole legibility on vision-language-action models that have to deliver on the silhouette.
