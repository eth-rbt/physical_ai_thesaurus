Intensity here is a decision, not a byproduct: each system was authored either to ask for attention or to dodge it. The low end is all calm-technology research and ambient displays that live in the periphery — the Dangling String twitching to traffic, the Ambient Orb glowing a feed, ambientROOM signalling through light and rippling water, Little Signals demoting a notification to a puff of air — none of which addresses you at all. At the top the object turns into something you can't sidestep, and it splits in two: creatures that solicit you (aibo and LOVOT begging to be played with and held, Be the Beat demanding you dance) versus enclosure, where the Apple Vision Pro simply takes your whole field of view. The clarifying case is the middle — the rule-based Nabaztag has no intelligence yet still wiggles and talks for you, while Ballie packs an LLM but rolls after you and mostly waits — proof that how loudly a thing performs is unhooked from how smart it is.
