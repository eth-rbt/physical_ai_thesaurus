Read across this set, intensity tracks almost perfectly with whether a system was authored to ask for attention or to avoid it: the low end is dominated by calm-technology research and passive home tech that lives in the periphery — a Dangling String twitching to network traffic, an Ambient Orb glowing weather, Little Signals nudging at the edge of awareness, Philips Hue and a self-tuning Nest thermostat that adapt without ever addressing you — while the high end is where embedded AI turns the object into a social actor that performs at you: Be the Beat, the expressive LLM-driven Moxie, and creaturely aibo and LOVOT that solicit cuddling and play. The surprise is the middle, where ai-embedded voice agents (Alexa reminders, the Apple Watch's haptic taps) and the proto-creatures Nabaztag and the shoulder-riding Stochastic Parrot show that demanding attention is a design choice independent of intelligence — Nabaztag has none and still wiggles for you, while Alexa has plenty and mostly waits to be summoned.
