Context-aware computing descends from Weiser's 1991 vision of machines that adapt to us and recede, made concrete by Olivetti's Active Badge (1992), whose infrared tags routed calls to whatever room you were in. PARC's ParcTab and Georgia Tech's Cyberguide turned location into a live input for filtering information and guiding tours, and the idea reached the mainstream with Google Now (2012), which fused location, calendar, and search history to surface answers before you asked — defining the ambient, proactive push model.
