Ambient computing is the calm-technology branch of ubicomp: information felt at the periphery rather than demanded through a screen. Weiser and Brown's principle found its canonical embodiment in the Dangling String (1995) — a cord twitching with network traffic — and the Tangible Media Group's ambientROOM, before Ambient Devices shipped the idea as the glowing Ambient Orb and Nabaztag's signalling rabbit. The lineage then folded into the mass market through self-effacing infrastructure (the Nest thermostat, Hue lighting, the Nest Hub's idle ambient mode) and was explicitly revived by Google's Little Signals (2022).
