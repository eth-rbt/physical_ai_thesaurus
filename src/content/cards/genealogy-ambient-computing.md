The quietest fact about this lineage is that almost none of it runs AI: the Dangling String is a motor wired to an Ethernet tap, the Ambient Orb maps one feed to one color, Pinwheels just spin faster — the legibility is mechanical, not intelligent. From Weiser's 1991 principle the idea passes through art (the Dangling String), into a sustained research program at the Tangible Media Group (Tangible Bits, the ambientROOM, Pinwheels, and Lumen extending it into rising, glowing shape), then out to market as the Orb and the signalling Nabaztag rabbit. Two endpoints frame the tension: Google's Little Signals (2022) reopens the original calm-technology brief almost unchanged, while the Nest Hub shows what the mass market actually accepted — not an object that was never a screen, but a screen taught to recede when no one is looking.
