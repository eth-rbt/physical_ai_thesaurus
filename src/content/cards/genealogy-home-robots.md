The only robot that ever truly moved into the home arrived as a hockey puck that vacuums: the Trilobite and then the Roomba justified their presence by doing one dull chore, and Roborock's LiDAR-mapped, AI-vision successors show how far that single beachhead can be pushed. Every other branch is still negotiating its welcome — arm-bearing helpers reach for the fetch-and-tidy work that would actually earn their keep, from the research-bench PR2 through Toyota's HSR to Hello Robot's carryable Stretch; rolling assistants (Astro, Ballie) try to turn the smart speaker into an ambient roommate; and creaturely companions (aibo, LOVOT) ask to be loved rather than useful, while Pepper bets a whole humanoid on it. Read together they reveal the home as the hardest room to enter: Dunne & Raby's speculative Technological Dreams robots — needy, neurotic, strange — warn that a machine which lives with you is a relationship, not an appliance.
