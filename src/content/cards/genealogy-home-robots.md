Home robots split into branches that all chase the same dream of a machine that lives with you. The utilitarian line begins with the Roomba's single-task floor cleaning and climbs through Roborock and Deebot toward rolling assistants like Astro, Ballie, and CLOi that try to be ambient presences; alongside it runs a creaturely companion line — aibo, LOVOT, Moxie, Jibo — built for attachment rather than chores, and an assistive-manipulation line — Toyota's HSR and Hello Robot's Stretch — reaching for arms that can actually fetch and tidy.
