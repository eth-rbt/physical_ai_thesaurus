The only robot that ever truly moved into the home arrived as a hockey puck that vacuums: the Roomba justified its presence by doing one dull chore, and Roborock's LiDAR-mapped, AI-vision successors show how far that single beachhead can be pushed. Every other branch is still negotiating its welcome — rolling assistants (Astro, Ballie, CLOi) try to turn the smart speaker into an ambient roommate, creaturely companions (aibo, LOVOT, Moxie, Jibo) ask to be loved rather than useful, and arm-bearing helpers (Toyota's HSR, Hello Robot's Stretch) reach for the fetch-and-tidy work that would actually earn their keep. Read together they reveal the home as the hardest room to enter: Dunne & Raby's speculative Technological Dreams robots — needy, neurotic, strange — and Moxie's abrupt cloud shutdown both warn that a machine which lives with you is a relationship, not an appliance.
