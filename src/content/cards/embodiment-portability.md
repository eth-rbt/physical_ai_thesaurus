Sorted by who supplies the motion, the set splits three ways: things bolted in place (the Unimate arm, Little Signals tapping from a shelf), things you carry that propel nothing themselves (AirPods, OrCam clipped to glasses, the Rabbit R1 in a pocket), and things that move under their own power — Astro by wheel, Spot by leg over terrain wheels can't take, Waymo across a whole city, BitDrones lofting into open air. What's striking is that intelligence and locomotion don't track together: OrCam runs core vision AI yet only repoints as your head turns, while the freely flying BitDrones are scripted from motion-capture with no onboard smarts and the city-crossing Waymo and floor-bolted Unimate sit at opposite ends entirely. How far something roams is a fact about its shell, not the mind inside it.
