Sorted by who does the moving, the set splits three ways: things that stay put and wait to be addressed (the wall-fixed Dangling String, a Nest Hub on the counter, an Echo on the shelf), things that ride along on the user (AirPods in the ear, an Apple Watch on the wrist, a Humane Pin and Ray-Ban Meta glasses worn on the body, a Rabbit R1 in the pocket), and things that propel themselves through space (a Roomba bound to the floor, an Astro roaming room to room, a Waymo carrying you across the city, BitDrones lifting interface elements into the air). The AI-embedded split tracks this loosely rather than cleanly — the freshest, most agentic models cluster on the worn and roaming bodies (the LLM-driven Rabbit R1 and Ray-Ban glasses, the deep-learning Waymo) while the most mobile research piece, BitDrones, carries no onboard intelligence at all — so portability and intelligence are decoupled: moving with you or roaming the room is a property of the shell, not of the smarts inside it.
