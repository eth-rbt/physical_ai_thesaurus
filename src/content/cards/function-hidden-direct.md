Read along visibility and the same domestic intelligence keeps changing how loudly it announces itself: SmartThings and a Nest thermostat dissolve into wiring and a wall plate, learning your patterns with almost nothing to notice, while Philips Hue and the deliberately low-key ambient research — the twitching Dangling String, the peripheral light and sound of ambientROOM — push information to the edge of attention by design. The middle is where the system starts to perform its labor: an Alexa that answers out loud, a Roomba audibly trundling across the floor. At the overt end the body becomes the interface — Ring's lit, watching doorbell, Jibo swivelling to face you, a Waymo with no driver at all, and Sophia's expressive humanoid face — and the AI-embedded lens tracks the same arc, since the hidden end leans on rule-based or classical learning baked into infrastructure (SmartThings, Nest, the calm-tech prototypes carry no real model) while the loud, social end is exactly where the large language and deep-learning models cluster (Alexa, Jibo, Sophia), as if intelligence only gets a face once it has something to say.
