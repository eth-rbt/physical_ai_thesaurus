Walk the visibility axis and the same act of sensing keeps getting louder: Weiser's ubicomp vision and Berkeley's smart dust set the hidden pole as doctrine — computing dissolved into the world until it physically disappears — and SmartThings spreads a whole home's logic across hubs and cloud rules with no body to point at. The calm-tech middle keeps information at the edge of attention on purpose: ambientROOM's rippling light, the spinning Pinwheels, the Ambient Orb's one glowing color, the faceless Automower visibly working the lawn. Only at the far end does the agent grow a body and start performing — Nabaztag's wiggling ears and voice, Ballie rolling after you and talking, Sophia's lifelike face holding your gaze. The AI-embedded split runs along the same line: the hidden end carries no model or bare rule-based logic baked into infrastructure, while real learned perception and language cluster at the loud, social end — as if intelligence only bothers growing a face once it has something to show.
