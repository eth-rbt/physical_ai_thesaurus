Everything that actually finishes a chore here has surrendered breadth for a fixed body: Cochrane's 1886 dishwasher, the June Oven on its counter, Roborock on the floor, the Automower on the lawn — each works because it does exactly one thing and nothing else. Cross over to general hands and the whole set falls back to demos and labs: Bot Handy pours wine only at CES, Stretch and Toyota's HSR help one teleoperated person, the PR2 folded laundry at $400k, and π0 learns table-bussing as a research policy. The arc from 1886 to a 2024 foundation model traces a single stubborn trade — the wider a machine reaches, the less of the housework it reliably gets done.
