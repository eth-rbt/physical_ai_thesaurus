The bodies range from things that vanish onto you — a camera clipped to your glasses, a pendant on your chest, even DuoSkin's circuitry painted onto bare skin — to a self-driving car whose entire form is the vehicle, and at the far edge Smart Dust, motes so small the body disappears into the room. In between sit shapes you read at a glance: Furhat is a face with no legs built only to talk, aibo is a legged dog that does no work, while Tesla Optimus and Spot earn their humanoid and four-legged shapes by promising labor, and inFORM has no fixed shape at all, rising into whatever it needs. Form is the first thing you read off a system and it sets the expectation — a face invites talk, legs promise terrain, a tattoo promises to disappear — before the AI acts at all, and the body can mislead as easily as inform: OrCam's eyewear hides the only genuinely AI-core system here, while several of the most committed-looking forms run no learned intelligence at all.
