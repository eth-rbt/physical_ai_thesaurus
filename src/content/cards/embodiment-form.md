The bodies range from things that vanish onto you — eyewear, a clip-on pin, even DuoSkin's circuitry painted onto bare skin — to a self-driving car whose entire form is the vehicle, and inFORM's pin surface that has no fixed shape at all but rises into whatever it needs. In between sit the social bodies that exist mainly to be read: Jibo swivels on a desktop, Furhat is a face with no legs, aibo is a dog that does no work, while Figure 02 and Spot earn their humanoid and four-legged shapes by doing labor. Form is the first thing you read off a system, and it sets the expectation — a face invites talk, legs promise terrain, a tattoo promises to disappear — before the AI acts at all.
