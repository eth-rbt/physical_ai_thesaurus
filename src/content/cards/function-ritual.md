Read together, these projects show that physical AI rarely earns a place in a home on raw capability — it earns it by attaching to a recurring moment: Apple Watch and the Oura Ring turn waking and moving into a daily score to close, Philips Hue and the Nest thermostat fold themselves into evening and bedtime rhythm, Roomba runs as a scheduled chore, and June bends cooking around its own routine, while Alexa and the Nest Hub anchor the morning brief. The AI-embedded split is the surprise: the most ritualized objects (Roomba, Hue, the early Nest) carry little or no on-device intelligence and run on schedules and rules, whereas the device built most explicitly around ritual — ElliQ, with its proactive greeting and bedtime check-ins (initiative 5) — and the day-summarizing Limitless Pendant are the ones leaning hardest on large language models, suggesting that becoming a household ritual is more a matter of reliable timing and companionship than of intelligence.
