Each of these claims a specific slot in the day and returns to it: the Oura Ring and Apple Watch own waking and movement, Google Now and the Nabaztag rabbit deliver the morning brief, June bends dinnertime around its camera, the Nest thermostat and Nest Hub fold into the bedtime hour, and the Limitless Pendant takes the wind-down. The AI-embedded split is the surprise. The objects most fused into ritual — the rule-based Nabaztag, the AI-free Tamagotchi that demands feeding or dies — carry little or no intelligence and run on timing and obligation, while the two leaning hardest on large language models, ElliQ (engineered around proactive greetings and check-ins, initiative 5) and the day-summarizing Pendant, suggest that becoming a habit is more about reliable presence than about being smart.
