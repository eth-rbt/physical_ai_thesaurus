Two clusters split the card by which question bites hardest. The fixed home sensors push who-else-can-reach-it: Ring hands doorstep clips to police, Nest Cam streams to Google and builds a named face library. The body-worn recorders push consent — WearCam, the eyewear-shaped Ray-Ban Meta, SenseCam firing on its own, Oura reading your body, the Limitless pendant transcribing everyone you meet — all sweep up people who never agreed. The telling thing is how little of the harm is AI: WearCam, SenseCam, MyLifeBits's whole-life archive, and the Active Badge all run no model at all; the recording itself is the exposure, and AI mostly adds reach (the face libraries, the I-XRAY dox of a stranger by glance). Smart Dust marks the limit — capture so small the device disappears — and Home Assistant the rebuttal: built local-first, it proves the cloud was always a choice.
