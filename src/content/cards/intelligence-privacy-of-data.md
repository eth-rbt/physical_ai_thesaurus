What unites these devices is that each turns part of life into a durable record; what divides them is who ends up holding it. The always-on home sensors (Ring films everyone at the door and has handed clips to police, Nest Cam builds a face library, Echo ships speech to Amazon's cloud even on false wakes) and the body-worn recorders (Ray-Ban Meta, Humane, Limitless) push the sharpest case: because Limitless transcribes every conversation and the glasses look like ordinary eyewear, the people captured are usually bystanders who never agreed. Against that current sit Apple Watch, which keeps much of its biometric sensing on-device, and Home Assistant, built local-first so the record stays on hardware you own — proof that the cloud is a design choice, not a requirement.
