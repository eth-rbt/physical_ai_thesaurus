What changes along this axis is what memory is *for*. At the short end it serves the next action and nothing more — Kinect tracks your skeleton only while you're in frame, Lens names a thing and discards it — and notably this is where the heaviest live AI runs. Move right and memory starts to mean a baseline you can be measured against: Pepper recalls this encounter, Nest Cam a rolling few days, Oura months of nightly readings precisely so it can flag what's off, Moflin's whole personality is the residue of weeks of handling. At the far pole memory stops being a feature and becomes the point — the Memex imagined it in 1945, Google Photos backed into it at billion-camera scale, the Limitless Pendant exists only to record you, and MyLifeBits shows the sharpest twist: the most total memory in the set ran on a plain database, not AI at all.
