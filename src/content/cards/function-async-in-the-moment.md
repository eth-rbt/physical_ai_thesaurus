Laid out by timing, the surprise is that the stakes invert as latency tightens. The async end is pure leisure: Limitless and Google Photos capture now so you can query whenever, Telescript dispatches an agent to report back on its own time, and the Husqvarna mower runs to a clock where the exact moment genuinely doesn't matter — and tellingly this end carries little embedded AI, just rules and classical learning. The middle is anticipation, acting a beat ahead of you (Nest staging warmth, Google Now surfacing the card, August unlocking as you arrive). But the real-time end is where the meaning flips: OrCam reads the world aloud the instant it sees it, AirPods duck and translate mid-sentence, Tacttoo pulses with no felt lag, and Waymo must avoid a collision in milliseconds — all of them leaning on core on-device perception, because here being slightly late stops being annoying and becomes dangerous.
