Laid out by timing, these projects reveal that temporal coupling, not capability, decides whether an AI feels useful, safe, or annoying. At the async end the work is detached from the moment: a Limitless Pendant and Google Photos quietly capture now so you can query later, while a Roborock or Husqvarna Automower runs to a clock you set and a Nest Thermostat anticipates ahead of you — here latency is a feature, and the AI ranges from none (the rule-based mower) to envisioned. The just-in-time middle is where the August Smart Lock unlocks exactly as you arrive and the Apple Watch fires a live health alert the instant it matters. At the hard real-time end, the split sharpens around embedded intelligence and stakes: Ray-Ban Meta glasses and AirPods translate and answer in the moment, OrCam MyEye reads the world aloud the instant it sees it, Tacttoo pulses on-skin haptics with no perceptible lag, and Waymo must avoid a collision in milliseconds — where being even slightly late stops being annoying and becomes dangerous.
